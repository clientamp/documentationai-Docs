This document proposes candidate names for the working-title products in the Client Amp suite. The goal is to establish a high-quality, serious naming convention that aligns with the existing locked products (**Lickity.click**, **TightEdit**, **TightShare**) and fits an integrated, AI-agent-first ecosystem.

## 1. Mac-First Course Publisher (Working: Studio)
*The flagship native macOS authoring application for video-first courses.*

| Candidate | Rationale | Tone | Possible Risks | Agent-First Fit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Studio** | Already established in branding; evokes a creative, professional environment. | Professional, Established | Generic; many "Studio" apps exist. | High. Agents operate "in the studio" alongside humans. |
| **Press** | Evokes the authority and finality of publishing; "going to press." | Serious, Classical | May feel "old-school" for a modern AI tool. | Moderate. Agents act as the "press crew." |
| **Compose** | Aligns with Mac-native terminology (Apple Mail); action-oriented. | Functional, Creative | Very common term in software. | High. Agents help "compose" content structure. |

## 2. Thumbnail Automation Tool (Working: Optic)
*A high-energy tool for A/B testing thumbnail variants and psychological hooks.*

| Candidate | Rationale | Tone | Possible Risks | Agent-First Fit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Arena** | Established in branding; visual, precise, and technical. | Sharp, Technical | A bit cold. | High. Agents provide the "Arena" on performance. |
| **Glimpse** | Thumbnails are a "glimpse" into the content; catchy and short. | Modern, Playful | Could be seen as "lightweight." | High. Agents generate "glimpses" for human review. |

## 3. Helm (Working: HelmNative)
*Integrated task and project management (Todo) system within the authoring app.*

| Candidate | Rationale | Tone | Possible Risks | Agent-First Fit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Helm** | Drops "Native" for a cleaner feel; implies control and steering. | Strong, Direct | Common metaphor. | High. Agents help "steer" the project via the Helm. |
| **Tack** | Sharp, minimalist; refers to "pinning" tasks (thumbtack) and speed. | Action-Oriented | May sound "small" or insignificant. | Excellent. Agents "tack" tasks to the workspace. |
| **Tempo** | Focuses on the rhythm and pace of tasks, especially recurring ones. | Rhythmic, Steady | Abstract. | High. Agents maintain the "tempo" of the workflow. |

## 4. Patron (Working: Sponsor CRM)
*A specialized CRM for managing sponsor relationships and campaign performance.*

| Candidate | Rationale | Tone | Possible Risks | Agent-First Fit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Patron** | Classical, respectful term for sponsors; implies a value-exchange. | Serious, Elite | Can feel a bit "old-world." | High. Agents manage the "Patron" database. |
| **Bond** | Focuses on the strength and security of the relationship/contract. | Strong, Reliable | Vague without context. | High. Agents monitor the health of the "Bond." |
| **Pulse** | Focuses on the real-time metrics and health of sponsor performance. | Active, Analytical | Sounds more like an analytics tool. | Excellent. Agents track the "Pulse" of the campaign. |

## 5. Conduit (Working: Flow)
*The media asset processing engine (import, processing, transcription).*

| Candidate | Rationale | Tone | Possible Risks | Agent-First Fit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Relay** | Implies the handoff of assets between various AI and processing stages. | Collaborative | Suggests a delay or sequence. | High. Agents "relay" assets through the pipeline. |
| **Conduit** | An industrial, functional metaphor for a data/media pipeline. | Functional, Robust | A bit technical/dry. | Excellent. Agents act as the "Conduit" for assets. |
| **Flux** | Implies constant movement and transformation of media data. | Dynamic, Modern | Can imply instability. | High. Agents manage the "Flux" of incoming media. |

| Candidate | Rationale | Tone | Possible Risks | Agent-First Fit |

| **Dash** | Short for "Dashboard"; implies speed and immediate utility. | Speedy, Minimalist | Very common. | High. Agents populate the "Dash" with insights. |
| **Bit** | Minimalist; implies the smallest unit of content/data. | Tech-Forward | Abstract. | Excellent. Agents handle the "Bits" for the user. |
| **Drop** | Simple, action-oriented; fits the "Droplet" heritage. | Clean, Direct | May sound like "dropping" an item. | High. Agents prepare the "Drops" for use. |

*The central "Single Source of Truth" for design system tokens and shared data.*

| Candidate | Rationale | Tone | Possible Risks | Agent-First Fit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Source** | Authoritative; the absolute origin of all styles and data. | Definitive, Core | Generic. | Excellent. Agents query the "Source" for truth. |
| **Prism** | Visual metaphor for design (splitting light/color); fits with "Arena." | Elegant, Visual | Abstract. | High. Agents use the "Prism" to distribute styles. |
| **Base** | Foundation for the entire integrated suite. | Stable, Solid | Common. | High. Everything is built on the "Base." |

## 8. SharedCore (Working: SharedCore)
*The shared libraries and utilities used across the entire tool suite.*

| Candidate | Rationale | Tone | Possible Risks | Agent-First Fit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Kernel** | A system-level, technical term for the core of an OS. | Industrial, Precise | Highly technical/geeky. | High. Agents interact with the system "Kernel." |
| **Nexus** | The central connection point where all products intersect. | Strategic | A bit "corporate." | Excellent. Agents link products at the "Nexus." |
| **Core** | Retains the working title's essence; simple and essential. | Foundational | Very generic. | High. The "Core" of the agent's logic. |

## Recommended "Suite" Archetype
Given the existing **Tight** family (**TightEdit**, **TightShare**), the most cohesive approach for the suite is **"The Precision Suite."**

**Proposed Lead Candidates:**
- **Studio** (Authoring)
- **Arena** (Thumbnails)
- **Helm** (Tasks)
- **Patron** (CRM)
- **Conduit** (Media)
- **Dash** (Assets)
- **Source** (Design)
- **Nexus** (Infrastructure)
