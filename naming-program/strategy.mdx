## 2. Naming Criteria (Weighted)

Each candidate name is scored against these six criteria:

| Criterion | Weight | What to evaluate |
|---|---|---|
| **Domain Availability** | 25% | .com or memorable TLD available? Cost? Clean history? |
| **Trademark Risk** | 20% | Is the name distinctive? Any marks in Class 009 or 042? |
| **Memorability** | 20% | Can a user recall and spell it after hearing it once? |
| **AI-Agent-First Fit** | 15% | Does it suggest automation or intelligence without using "AI-"? |
| **Cross-Product Cohesion** | 10% | Does it fit alongside Lickity.click and TightEdit? |
| **Future Flexibility** | 10% | Can the name stretch if product scope expands? |

**Note:** Names scoring below 3.0 overall are eliminated.

---

## 3. Brand Tone & Guidelines

The tone is anchored by the three locked names: **Fast, sharp, and slightly fun.**

- **Lickity.click** — playful, fast-sounding, uses novelty TLDs as brand identity.
- **TightEdit/TightShare** — compound words, action verb + modifier, implies precision.
- **The "Tight" prefix** is reserved for the video editing sub-family. Do not extend to unrelated products.
- **Avoid generic AI/tech suffixes** (.ai, -ly, -ify) unless the resulting name is genuinely distinctive.

---

## 4. Screening Process (The Checklist)

Before a name is finalized, it must pass the [Naming Screening Checklist](checklist).

1. **Digital Identity:** Domain and social handle availability.
2. **The "Google Test":** Searchability and SEO unique results.
3. **Legal Screening:** USPTO and WIPO trademark quick-scans.
4. **Pronunciation:** The "Phone Test" and "Slacker Test" (2 syllables).
5. **Internationalization:** Checking for accidental meanings or offensive slang.
