---
title: Naming Screening Checklist
description: Practical, repeatable process for screening product name candidates.
---

This document provides a practical, repeatable process for screening product name candidates across the Client Amp suite. The goal is identify weak or risky names early, before investing in branding, design, or development.

---

## 1. Digital Identity & Domain Access

**Primary Goal:** Can users find you, and do you own the "home" of the product?

- [ ] **Exact Match .com:** Is `[name].com` available or affordable? 
- [ ] **Modern TLDs (.app, .io, .ai):** If `.com` is taken, are these available? (Avoid `.net` or `.org` for commercial products).
- [ ] **Social Handles:** Check availability on **X/Twitter**, **LinkedIn**, and **GitHub** for the exact name or `get[name]`.
- [ ] **App Store / Mac App Store:** Search for existing apps with the same name. (Critical for native products like `HelmNative` and `TightEdit`).

## 2. The "Google Test" (Search & SEO)

**Primary Goal:** How unique is the name in the current digital landscape?

- [ ] **Zero-Result Search:** Search the name in quotes (`"[name]"`). Do you get millions of generic results or a handful of specific ones?
- [ ] **Top Competition:** Who owns the top 5 results for the unquoted name? (Avoid names that compete with massive established brands or generic terms like "Flow" or "Sponsor").
- [ ] **Auto-Correct Risk:** Does Google try to "fix" the name to something else? (e.g., "Lickity" vs "Lickety").
- [ ] **Keyword Overlap:** Is the name a "head term" in SEO (e.g., "Email Client")? If so, you'll never rank for it.

## 3. Trademark & Legal Screening (Red Flags)

*Disclaimer: This is a screening tool, not legal advice.*

- [ ] **USPTO (TESS) Search:** Search the name in the **USPTO Trademark Electronic Search System**. Look for "Live" marks in **Class 009 (Software)** or **Class 042 (SaaS)**.
- [ ] **WIPO Global Brand Database:** Search for international conflicts if the product has a global audience.
- [ ] **Common Law Conflicts:** Search Google for `"[name] software"` or `"[name] app"`. If someone is using it for a similar purpose, even without a trademark, it's a risk.
- [ ] **Direct Competitor Proximity:** Is the name too close to a competitor's name? (e.g., `Slack` vs `Stack`).

## 4. Pronunciation & Meaning

**Primary Goal:** Is it easy to share and "safe" for a global audience?

- [ ] **The "Phone Test":** Can you say the name over a noisy phone line and have the other person spell it correctly on the first try?
- [ ] **The "Slacker Test":** Is it two syllables? (Two is usually the "sweet spot" for memorability).
- [ ] **International "Oops":** Does the name mean something offensive, awkward, or unintended in major languages (Spanish, French, German, Mandarin)?
- [ ] **Visual Balance:** Does it look good in lowercase? (e.g., `lickityclick` vs `helmnative`).

## 5. Suite Cohesion (The "Client Amp" Fit)

**Primary Goal:** Does it feel like it belongs to the same family of tools?

- [ ] **Verb/Action Driven:** Does it follow the pattern of `TightEdit` or `TightShare` (Action + State)?
- [ ] **Modern/Playful:** Does it follow the pattern of `Lickity.click`?
- [ ] **AI-Agent Ready:** Does the name feel "smart" or "automated" without being cliché (avoiding `[Name]AI`)?
- [ ] **Standalone Strength:** Could this name survive if it wasn't part of the Client Amp suite?

## 6. Decision Matrix (1-5 Scale)

Evaluate the name on a scale of 1 (Poor) to 5 (Excellent):

| Criteria | Score | Notes |

## 7. Next Steps After Screening

1. **Shortlist:** Move names with a Total Score > 15 to the next phase.
2. **Dave's Gut Check:** Present the top 3 candidates for intuition-based selection.
3. **Formal Filing:** Once a name is selected, initiate formal trademark filing before public launch.
